FAQ

Questions merchants ask before they apply

Stripe, Creem, or PayPal rejected me without even asking questions. Will the same thing happen here?

Auto-rejection is common if you sell AI tools, digital products, or run a community platform — risk systems often decline on category alone, before anyone looks at what you actually sell. We review manually: product, checkout flow, pricing, refund policy, delivery model. That doesn't guarantee approval. It means the decision is based on your business, not a merchant category code.

What exactly do you look at before approving an account?

Your website, checkout flow, target markets, expected volume, payment methods, support process, and dispute exposure — the same information any processor should want before taking you on. We just ask upfront, before a chargeback spike forces the conversation.

I sell AI-generated images or AI writing tools — is that too risky for you?

Not automatically. AI products fall outside standard category codes, which is exactly the gap we work in. We look at your delivery model, refund policy, and how customers actually use the product — not the label on the box.

How long does the review take?

It depends on complexity. A straightforward SaaS subscription clears faster than a multi-market community payment setup. If something in your application needs fixing first, we tell you early instead of leaving you waiting on a decision that was never coming.

Does approval mean I just get a merchant account and that's it?

No — approval is the start. Once you're live, you get hosted checkout, payment links, Shopify and WooCommerce integration, a REST API for custom builds, AI usage billing, community payments, multi-currency settlement, and chargeback alerts.

Can I use this with Shopify or WooCommerce, or do I need to rebuild my checkout?

Both paths work. Already on Shopify or WooCommerce? We integrate with what you have. Need something custom — usage-based billing for an AI product, payments inside a Telegram or Discord community? REST API and payment links cover that too.

My business doesn't fit a normal category code. Is that a problem?

That's most of who we work with. Category codes were never built for AI usage billing, paid Discord communities, or cross-border digital subscriptions — which is usually why a standard processor declines without a real look. We evaluate what the business does, not which code it's supposed to fit.

Am I setting this up myself, or do I work with an actual person?

A real operator, not a support ticket queue. The person who reviews your application stays on through go-live, settlement, disputes, and any account changes after.

What happens if my dispute rate rises or something changes later — will my account just get shut down?

We can't promise nothing ever changes — payment risk moves. What we can promise is how you hear about it: if your dispute rate rises or something needs attention, a person tells you, with time to respond, instead of a frozen account telling you first.
